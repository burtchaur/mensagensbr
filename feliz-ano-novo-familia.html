Feliz Ano Novo Familia: Celebrating The New Year With Loved Ones

As we bid farewell to the old year and welcome the new, it's time to celebrate with our nearest and dearest. Feliz ano novo familia, or Happy New Year Family, is a time-honored tradition in Brazil where families come together to ring in the new year with joy, love, and hope for the future. In this article, we'll explore the customs and traditions associated with feliz ano novo familia, and how you can make the most of this special time with your loved ones.

History and Origins of Feliz Ano Novo Familia

Feliz ano novo familia has its roots in the ancient Roman festival of Saturnalia, which was celebrated in honor of the god Saturn. During this festival, which took place in late December, people would exchange gifts, decorate their homes with greenery, and enjoy feasting and merrymaking. In Christian times, the festival was adapted into Christmas, but many of the old pagan customs remained.

In Brazil, feliz ano novo familia is celebrated on New Year's Eve, or Reveillon, which is a time of great joy and celebration. Families come together to share a meal, exchange gifts, and watch fireworks displays. It's a time to reflect on the past year, and to look forward to the new one with hope and optimism.

Preparing for Feliz Ano Novo Familia

Preparing for feliz ano novo familia is a big part of the celebration. Families will often spend weeks planning the perfect meal, decorating the house, and buying gifts for their loved ones. Many families will also attend church services on New Year's Eve, to give thanks for the blessings of the past year, and to pray for a happy and prosperous new year.

One of the most important aspects of preparing for feliz ano novo familia is choosing the right outfit. Brazilians take great pride in their appearance, and New Year's Eve is a time to dress up in your finest clothes. Many people will wear white, which is considered to be a lucky color, while others will wear brightly colored clothes to reflect the festive mood.

The New Year's Eve Meal

The New Year's Eve meal is a highlight of feliz ano novo familia. Families will often prepare a feast of traditional Brazilian dishes, such as feijoada, a hearty stew made with black beans and pork, or moqueca, a fish stew made with coconut milk and palm oil. Other popular dishes include roasted meats, rice, and beans, and a variety of desserts such as brigadeiros, beijinhos, and pudim de leite.

Drinks are also an important part of the meal, with many families serving champagne or sparkling wine to toast the new year. In some parts of Brazil, it's traditional to drink a glass of champanhe com romã, or champagne with pomegranate seeds, which is said to bring good luck.

Decorating for Feliz Ano Novo Familia

Decorating the house is another important aspect of feliz ano novo familia. Many families will decorate their homes with flowers, balloons, and streamers in bright colors such as yellow, green, and blue, which are considered to be lucky colors. It's also traditional to decorate the house with garlands of garlic, which are said to ward off evil spirits.

Celebrating Feliz Ano Novo Familia

Once the preparations are complete, it's time to celebrate feliz ano novo familia with your loved ones. Many families will attend midnight Mass at their local church, to welcome the new year in prayer. Others will gather at home, to share a meal and watch the fireworks displays that are a hallmark of New Year's Eve in Brazil.

One of the most important traditions of feliz ano novo familia is the custom of making wishes for the new year. At midnight, when the clock strikes twelve, it's traditional to make a wish for the coming year, and to throw handfuls of lentils or rice into the air, which is said to bring good luck and prosperity.

Fireworks Displays

Fireworks displays are a big part of New Year's Eve celebrations in Brazil, and many cities will have organized displays that can be seen from miles around. In Rio de Janeiro, for example, the Copacabana beach fireworks display is one of the largest in the world, attracting millions of visitors each year.

Many families will also set off their own fireworks displays, lighting up the night sky with colorful rockets and sparklers. It's important to be safe when setting off fireworks, however, and to follow all the necessary precautions to avoid accidents.

Conclusion

Feliz ano novo familia is a time to come together with loved ones, to celebrate the passing of the old year and the arrival of the new. Whether you attend church services, share a meal, or watch the fireworks displays, the most important thing is to do it with joy and love in your heart. So from our family to yours, feliz ano novo familia, and may the coming year bring you all the happiness and prosperity you deserve.
